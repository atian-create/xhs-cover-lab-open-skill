# Cover Archetypes

Use this file to classify a Xiaohongshu / RedNote cover into one primary type.

## 1. Face-Led Big-Keyword Cover

Signals:

- face or upper body is the trust anchor
- huge keyword or result text
- expression, identity, or authority adds the click reason

Works for:

- creator operation, AI, career, education, commentary, parenting, personal IP

Learn:

- big keyword placement
- expression and title alignment
- split screen or proof scene

Do not copy:

- face, expression, identity, authority, camera confidence, speaking ability

## 2. Person Plus Proof Scene

Signals:

- person plus tool screen, editing timeline, product, food, classroom, street,
  desk, or workflow proof
- the scene proves the claim

Works for:

- AI tools, tutorials, creator workflow, product demos, local guide, food review

Learn:

- put the result next to the person
- show output, screen, tool, product, or scene

Avoid:

- empty selfie with no proof

## 3. No-Person Knowledge Card

Signals:

- no face
- clear card structure
- headline, checklist, steps, table, comparison, or workflow

Works for:

- tutorials, frameworks, AI/Agent explainers, career lists, education content

Learn:

- one page one job
- keyword visible in one second
- modules instead of paragraphs

Avoid:

- making it look like a boring Word table

## 4. Text-Dense Screenshot Dry-Good Cover

Signals:

- looks like article screenshot, memo, X/Twitter thread, Weibo post, public
  account page, or long-form text card
- dense information but highlighted keywords

Works for:

- AI workflows, creator lessons, female growth opinions, industry observations,
  long drafts, transcripts

Learn:

- extract keywords before design
- page one gives promise, page two proves substance

Avoid:

- raw screenshots with no hierarchy
- tiny unreadable text

## 5. Interaction Prompt Cover

Signals:

- one question designed to trigger comments
- simple background, emoji/sticker, highlighted trigger words, or staged
  comment/PPT screenshot

Works for:

- account activation, local life, food, travel, community topics, relationship,
  workplace, hobby discussion

Learn:

- ask a question tied to the account mainline
- make users want to open the comment section

Avoid:

- unrelated hot questions that bring noise but no followers
- fake screenshots or fake comments

## 6. Food / Local-Life Desire Cover

Signals:

- real food, store, city, street, material, menu, route, or place is the main
  visual
- city/place/price/route/judgment information is obvious

Works for:

- food, store visit, local life, weekend plan, city guide

Learn:

- real scene plus one clear judgment
- local keyword, price, route, old shop, avoid-pitfall

Do not copy:

- lighting, plating, camera skill, store access, city resource

## 7. Travel / City Information Cover

Signals:

- destination, route, budget, time, first-time guide, avoid-pitfall, landmark
- users save because it helps planning

Works for:

- travel guide, local tourism, city walk, transport, itinerary

Learn:

- make "where / how much / how long / worth it" visible
- combine visual desire with practical info

Avoid:

- pretty scenery without decision value

## 8. Good-Product / Product Scene Cover

Signals:

- product appears where it is used: hand, bathroom, kitchen, desk, suitcase,
  room, travel scene
- cover creates association between product and life problem

Works for:

- good products, beauty, home, travel gear, digital products, productized offers

Learn:

- product plus usage scene
- before/after, "why did no one tell me earlier", one clear use case

Do not copy:

- polished hands/nails, expensive home, rare product access, lifestyle texture

## 9. Room-As-Identity Lifestyle Cover

Signals:

- room, desk, books, bed, closet, devices, notes, or small space proves the
  creator's lifestyle
- title names a life-stage narrative: 30+, solo living, independence, reading,
  freedom, "I can also live like this"

Works for:

- female growth, solo living, reading, digital setup, lifestyle, self-consistency

Learn:

- repeated room angle
- objects as memory anchors
- scene as proof of choices

Do not copy:

- real room, devices, money/free time, reading habit, personal story

## 10. List / Collection Information Cover

Signals:

- "5 个 / 10 个 / 一篇看完 / 清单 / 宝藏 / 信息源"
- users save because someone filtered information for them

Works for:

- AI tools, city stores, travel spots, products, creators, books, courses

Learn:

- clear count
- clear audience
- clear reason to save

Avoid:

- generic lists with no filtering standard

## 11. High-Production Cinematic Cover

Signals:

- film-like color, camera language, strong scene, actor performance, location
- comments often praise production quality

Works for:

- Vlog, documentary-style content, high-production lifestyle, travel films,
  creator stories

Learn:

- story arc
- opening frame
- emotional rhythm

Do not copy:

- equipment, team, lighting, editing, acting, location, time cost
